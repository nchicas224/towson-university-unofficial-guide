My first semester at Towson University has been full of challenges and great experiences. Living in the dorms has been a nice surprise. My roommate and I get along well, even though we had never met before. We respect each other’s space and have separate friends, which helped me meet more people.

There are lots of food options on campus, but they’re expensive. I spent more than I expected on food early on but started eating at the dining facility later because it’s cheaper and decent.

This semester was tough being away from home for the first time, but I could visit when I needed a break. It was harder because I got sick three times with tonsil and adenoid issues, taking almost a week to recover each time. I struggled to keep up with classwork but recently had surgery over the break and feel more motivated.

Overall, I’ve had a great time making friends, meeting amazing staff, going to football games, and working out at the gym. I’m excited for what’s next!

Towson is a pretty good school. It is an open campus, so you’re able to access a lot of things. The mall is about a 20 minute walk from campus, and there is a shopping center about 30 minutes away. The professors are a hit or miss. You should check rate my professor before choosing one, but they aren’t always accurate or on there. As a psychology major, finding a professor on rate my professor or even getting into a class is pretty hard. If you want to major in psychology, check out the school before coming. The buildings are super big and nice. And there are a lot of places where you can study. I would like it if the dining halls were a little better. The fruit and vegetables selection isn’t the best. I typically wait until going home to eat those, or supply myself with some in my fridge. The dorms are really good, big, and comfy. And if you have friends there is plenty of space in the room for them, even for sleepovers. If you prefer a cold room to sleep in, I recommend a fan.

I transferred to Towson my sophomore year of college and I enjoyed many aspects. It's a very organized university and continuously progressing. There was a lot of construction my first year as they were building a new science center that I was lucky enough to use my senior year. They also updated and upgraded the student center.

I was a commuter, so I don't have much experience with their party scene or housing. But, Towson does a good job of keeping everyone informed about the nature of the environment. There was several crimes committed on or surrounding the campus and the school would update everyone via text or email. The Towson area is okay, it's not the safest but not completely horrible either. There's a mall that's about a 10 min. walk away, a public library, a grocery store, and plenty of places to eat and shop.

I graduated from Towson with my Bachelors of Science in Biology and had no issues obtaining my degree and connecting with professors if I needed extra assistance.
